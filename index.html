🌫️   Winter · Nawabganj · Dhaka Chapter One

How it began

It wasn't planned. The best things never are.


A sudden trip to Nawabganj — cold winter air thick with fog, the fields pale and still, the kind of quiet that only villages get when the season turns. The kind of cold that makes you want to be close to someone. I didn't know yet that this trip would become the story I'd tell for the rest of my life.


She wasn't into me. But somewhere in the mist and the chai and the cold morning light, I was already completely, quietly in love with her.


I'm so glad we went.

Chapter Two

The day that changed everything

We never had an official first date. What we had was better — an entire day that just kept going, like neither of us wanted it to stop.

Late night
The Graveyard Walk
Walking at night past a graveyard, making jokes the whole way. Either very brave or very comfortable with each other. Definitely the second one.
Her home
Ludo, Monopoly & Pure Chaos
Ludo first. Then Monopoly — dhoni howar mojar khela. I laughed until it hurt. We weren't really playing to win. We were just happy to be in the same room.
[ Your best Monopoly moment — who cheated, who flipped out, who won? 🎲 ]
2AM kitchen
Noodles & Chaa
She made us noodles and chaa. Simple, warm, perfect. There's something quietly romantic about someone making food for you in the middle of the night.
All night
We Didn't Sleep
Stayed up all night. Talking, laughing, just being. I don't remember every word. I just remember not wanting any of it to stop.
Morning
Separated & Reunited
We moved to another house and got separated for a bit. Worst few minutes of the whole day. Then we found each other again — and everything felt lighter.
The river
The River & The Mud Incident 😭
She swam. I stood at the edge watching. Then getting up, I slipped in the wet mud. A full, spectacular, completely dignified slip. Mortifying. Worth it forever.
Flower field
I Picked Flowers for Her
We went to a flower field. I picked some for her and we walked together, laughing at nothing. She wasn't into me yet. I was completely, irrevocably in love — picking flowers like they were the most important things I'd ever held.
